## Critical Analysis

### What Went Right
- **Bearish engulfing exits freed re-entry**: Trades 1-2 got quick BE exits at small profit (+$176, +$0). This freed the bot to re-enter at 07:14 with a fresh position and MORE shares (7,142 vs 5,882) — catching a much bigger piece of the move.

- **Early detection**: Bot identified the setup at 07:02, just 2 minutes into the session, and entered at 07:03. Fast.
- **Pattern signals correct**: ABCD, ASC_TRIANGLE, FLAT_TOP, R2G, VOL_SURGE all fired — this was a legit breakout.
- **Net improvement**: Previous backtest (without BE exits) netted +$5,823. With BE exits matching live bot behavior, net improved to +$6,890 (+$1,067 better).

### What Went Wrong
- **Trade 4 (-$1,000, -1.0R)**: Entered the first pullback at $5.89 after the stock ran from $3.23 to $6.19. Stop at $5.57 was hit just one bar later. The stock V-recovered from $5.30 to $6.34 the very next bars (07:32-07:33). This was the right idea (pullback entry) but the stop was too tight for the volatility.
- **Topping wicky exit still early on Trade 3**: Exited at $4.68 but stock went to $6.19 (+32% more). The TW exit was mechanically correct but left profit on the table.

### What the Bot Missed — The Big Story
The bot went completely dark after 07:37 and missed the ENTIRE run from ~$5.50 to $12.93 (135% move over 3+ hours). Here's why:

1. **Circuit breaker obliterated state** (08:00-08:14): The LULD halts created bars with 100-200% ranges (e.g., $6.28 → $2.15 → $6.17 in a single minute). The bot's state machine had no framework for this chaos. After the halts cleared, the stock settled at $5.89 but the bot couldn't rebuild a structure.

2. **MACD stayed bearish for hours**: After the 07:35 MACD bearish cross, the indicator never recovered enough for the bot to re-engage. At 09:48, 10:47, and 11:12, MACD was still at -7.0 despite the stock being at $6.90, $7.90, and $10.76 respectively. The extreme volatility from 08:00-08:14 poisoned the MACD calculation.

3. **Topping wicky kept resetting**: At 09:47, the bot tried to re-engage but topping wicky immediately reset the structure.

4. **No market-open re-entry**: The 09:30 volume surge (2.2M, 1.5M, 3.0M in 3 bars) was a clear institutional re-entry signal. The stock broke above pre-halt levels. But the bot had no framework for "market open = fresh opportunity."

5. **Missed the staircase pattern**: From 09:30 to 11:18, VERO made a textbook staircase: $6.20 → $7.40 → $8.25 → $9.60 → $12.93, with pullbacks and consolidations between each step. Each step made new HOD. The bot saw none of this.
