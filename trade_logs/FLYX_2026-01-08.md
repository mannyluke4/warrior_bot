1. **Trade 1 entry timing was excellent**: Entered at $4.91 only 1 minute after scanner hit. This was BETTER than Ross's entry ($5.89). The bot was $0.98 cheaper and caught the initial impulse.

2. **MACD gate correctly avoided the bull trap**: At 08:49 and 08:54, MACD skip prevented entry into the $7.03 rejection that Ross also identified and avoided. Bot and Ross agreed on the same read here.

3. **Trade 4 TW exit was smart**: Quick +$473 in post-halt chop. The stock dropped to $6.31 after. Good defensive exit.

4. **BE exits limited damage on Trade 2**: Without BE exit, Trade 2 would have stopped at $4.92 for ~-$1,000 instead of -$397.

### What Went Wrong
1. **BE exit killed Trade 1**: Entered at $4.91, BE'd at $5.23. Stock went to $6.50. Lost +$2,090 potential on a 10s bar bearish engulfing that was just a normal pullback during a parabolic ramp. This is the same issue as ROLR Trade 3 and VERO Trade 1.

3. **Rapid-fire entries burned capital**: 3 trades in 13 minutes (08:16-08:29), net -$976. By the time the real breakout happened at 08:34, bot was already stopped out and in cooldown.

### What the Bot Missed
- **Ross's patience**: Ross said he "wasn't sure" about FLYX initially. He waited for the consolidation at $5.89 and the $6 level, entered on the micro pullback. Bot chased every impulse.
- **Conviction-based hold through dips**: The 08:30-08:33 dip to $5.61-$5.68 was normal consolidation. Ross held through it. Bot's mechanical stop at $5.69 couldn't distinguish between a dip and a reversal.
- **Ross's discipline to stop**: After taking profit, Ross "stepped away" — didn't trade the post-open rip to $8.88. Bot entered twice more (Trades 4 and 5).

## Key Takeaways

1. **BE exits during parabolic ramps are the #1 recurring issue**: FLYX Trade 1, ROLR Trade 3, VERO Trade 1 — all cut massive winners. The 10s bar bearish engulfing fires on normal pullbacks during strong moves.

2. **$0.01 stop tolerance matters**: Trade 3 stopped at $5.69, bar low was $5.68. Next bar ripped to $6.39. A $0.01 wider stop = +$1,333 potential instead of -$1,000.

3. **Bot's detection speed is actually good here**: 1 minute after scanner, better entry than Ross. The problem isn't detection — it's what happens AFTER entry.

4. **Patience > speed**: Ross waited 13 minutes (08:15 scanner → 08:28 entry). Bot entered in 1 minute. Ross's patience led to a cleaner entry with higher conviction.

5. **MACD gate correctly avoided the bull trap**: Both Ross and bot identified the $7 level as dangerous via MACD. This is a bot strength.

6. **Overtrading**: 5 trades on one stock in 3.75 hours vs Ross's 2 trades in 12 minutes. The cooldown/re-entry logic doesn't prevent overtrading — it just spaces it out.
