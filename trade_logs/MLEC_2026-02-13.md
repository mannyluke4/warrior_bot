### The BE Exit Tradeoff
The addition of bearish engulfing exits fundamentally changes the bot's behavior on MLEC:
- **Old behavior (no BE exits)**: 2 trades, late entries, -$2,513 net
- **New behavior (with BE exits)**: 6 trades, rapid in/out, +$478 net (no single trade captured more than +1.8R)
- **The swing**: Adding BE exits turned a -$2,513 loss into a +$478 gain. The key mechanism: early entries that would have been held through massive drawdowns now get BE exited quickly, freeing capital for re-entry. Trade 1's whipsaw exit at $7.32 enabled Trade 2's clean +1.8R capture.
- **The cost**: On a stock with extreme intrabar volatility like MLEC, 10-second BE exits also act as a "shakeout machine" — they exit on normal volatility that looks bearish at the 10s level but is just noise at the 1-minute level. The bot trades more but can't hold through consolidation to capture the really big moves.

### The Big Lesson: BE Exits Change Everything

This is a **fundamentally different result from the old backtest**:

| | ROLR | MLEC (old, no BE) | MLEC (new, with BE) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ross's approach** | Scale in/out 10+ times = $85K | Scale in/out 3-4 times = $43K | Scale in/out 3-4 times = $43K |
| **Bot's approach** | 5 trades, all-or-nothing = $1.3K | 2 trades = -$2,513 | 6 trades, rapid in/out = +$478 |
| **Who won?** | Ross by 65x | Ross completely | Ross by 90x |

With BE exits, the bot's behavior flipped from "late entry holder" to "rapid scalper." The old backtest (without BE exits in the backtester) had 2 trades with late entries that netted -$2,513. Now with BE exits matching live bot behavior, early entries get exited quickly and the bot re-enters — turning a loss into a small gain. The critical Trade 2 from $7.92 to $9.25 (+$1,821) was only possible because Trade 1's BE exit at $7.32 freed capital.

The core tension: BE exits on 10-second bars are too sensitive for stocks with 10%+ intrabar ranges — they exit on noise. But they also prevent the bot from holding through massive drawdowns, and the rapid re-entry mechanism can capture momentum that a late single entry misses.

### Profile Update: "Micro-Float Biotech Breakout" (Variant)

Compared to ROLR's "Micro-Float Parabolic Runner":
- **Similar**: Micro float, news catalyst, trigger bar >50%, immediate pullback, multi-leg structure

- **Key signal**: L2 spoof detection. Ross saw fake sell walls and still entered — he knew the spoofing was temporary. If the bot had L2 enabled, it could detect unusual sell pressure and factor it in
