### The Position Sizing Paradox

This case perfectly illustrates the bot's sizing dilemma:
- **Trade 1**: R = $0.10 → 9,244 shares → TOO BIG for the setup quality and liquidity
- **Trade 2**: R = $2.62 → 381 shares → TOO SMALL for the opportunity

The fixed $1K risk formula creates an inverse relationship between stop distance and position size. On explosive moves with wide stops, the bot is under-sized. On thin premarket setups with tight stops, the bot is over-sized. Ross doesn't have this problem because he sizes by conviction and capital, not by a formula.
