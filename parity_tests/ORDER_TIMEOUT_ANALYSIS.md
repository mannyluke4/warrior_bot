## Findings

The 4 orders tell a clear story:

| # | Symbol | Time | Entry | Actual Price | Gap | Root Cause |
|---|--------|------|-------|-------------|-----|------------|
| 1 | FCUV | 09:48 | $8.45 | $9.23 | **+$0.78** | Price already blew through — signal fired on seed data, not live |
| 2 | FCUV | 16:03 | $5.02 | $5.47 | **+$0.43** | Same — seed armed at $5.02 but stock was at $5.47 |
| 3 | MLEC | 16:07 | $10.02 | $11.60 | **+$1.56** | Same — seed armed at $10.02 but stock was at $11.60 |
| 4 | PRFX | 16:10 | $3.02 | $2.55 | **-$0.47** | Price BELOW entry — would have filled into a loss |

**The issue is NOT the limit price buffer ($0.02) or timeout (10 seconds).** The issue is the entry prices from the seed are stale — they reflect where the stock WAS during the seed period, not where it IS now. By the time the detector arms from seed replay and fires an entry signal, the actual market price is far away from the armed trigger level.

### Orders 1-3: Stale Arming After Seed Replay
The tick-level seed replays hours of historical ticks through the detector. When the detector arms at $8.45 (FCUV PM high from that morning), the stock has already moved to $9.23 in real time. The entry signal fires because the last seed tick crossed the trigger, but that crossing happened hours ago — it's not a live breakout.

### Order 4: Reverse Stale — Price Collapsed
PRFX armed at $3.02 (whole dollar level) from seed data. By the time the signal fired, the stock had dropped to $2.55. The order WOULD have filled — into a $0.47/share loss against the box. The timeout actually protected us here.

## Recommendations

1. **After seed replay completes, the detector should NOT fire entry signals on the final seed tick.** The seed's purpose is to warm up EMA/VWAP/vol baselines — NOT to generate live entry signals. If the detector arms during seeding, it should mark the armed state but wait for a LIVE tick to cross the trigger price before signaling entry.

2. **Add a `seed_complete` flag to the detector.** After seeding, set a flag that suppresses entry signals for the first N live ticks (or first 30 seconds). This gives the detector time to see current prices before deciding to enter.

3. **The $0.02 limit buffer and 10s timeout are fine.** These aren't the problem. In all 4 cases, the stock wasn't even close to the limit price. The entries were bad, not the order mechanics.

4. **Afternoon entries (16:00+) from seed replay are especially dangerous.** The seed replays the full day (4 AM - 4 PM), then the bot sees a 16:00 tick and the detector fires on patterns from hours ago. Consider gating: no entries from seed-triggered arms for the first 60 seconds of live data.
