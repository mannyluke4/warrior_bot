## Analysis & Next Steps

### What V2 Proved

1. **The overtrading fix works.** V1 took 184 trades in 11 days and lost 74%. V2 took 47 trades in 49 days and lost 25%. Same strategy, same market — the only difference is candidate selection. Fewer, better candidates = smaller losses.

2. **The score gate works but barely matters here.** Only 2 trades differed between A and B — both were small losses blocked by the gate ($244 saved). In top-5 ranked candidates, almost everything scores above 8.0. The gate is more useful when the candidate pool is wider.

3. **Volume-first ranking misses the asymmetric winners.** The biggest win in 49 days was +$597. The 2-week backtest had wins of +$8,360 (VERO), +$4,907 (BNAI), +$6,735 (GWAV). Those stocks either had low PM volume (BNAI: 5,686), weren't in the scanner (VERO), or didn't produce trades with these env settings (ROLR, GWAV). The ranking algorithm selects "obvious" high-volume stocks that everyone is watching — but the edge is in the less-obvious ones.

4. **Win rate (24%) and profit factor (0.27) are terrible.** Average win ($246) is slightly less than average loss ($290). The bot needs big winners to offset the many small losses — that's how the strategy is designed to work. Without the runners, it's a slow bleed.

### The Real Problem: Where Do The Winners Come From?

Looking at the 2-week backtest winners:
- **VERO** ($3.58→$4.68): Low-float stock, moderate gap. NOT in scanner.
- **BNAI** ($4.51→$5.62): Low PM volume (5,686). Filtered out by 50K minimum.
- **ROLR** ($9.33→$12.90): 10.6M PM volume, #1 by ranking. But armed 1, signaled 0 in the sim — no trades with current env settings.
- **GWAV** ($5→$8+): 1.5M PM volume, made top 5. Armed 1, signaled 0 — no trades.

Two of the four biggest winners can't even be FOUND by the scanner. The other two are found but don't produce trades with the current env config (they did in the 2-week backtest with different settings/dates).

### What Needs to Happen Next

**Problem 1: Scanner misses winners (VERO not found)**
- Investigate why VERO isn't in the Jan 16 scanner results
- May need to widen scanner criteria or add a "late discovery" rescan mechanism
- This is the single biggest gap — can't trade what you can't find

**Problem 2: Volume filter kills small-volume winners (BNAI filtered)**
- BNAI had only 5,686 PM volume on Jan 14 but was the 2nd biggest winner
- Consider lowering the PM volume floor or using a different metric
- Maybe "volume relative to float" matters more than absolute volume

**Problem 3: Top-volume stocks don't produce the right setups**
- High-volume stocks tend to be more efficient (tighter spreads, faster mean reversion)
- The micro-pullback setup works better on less-efficient stocks where retail flow drives momentum
- Consider inverting the ranking or using a "sweet spot" filter (moderate volume, not maximum)

**Problem 4: Sim settings may differ from 2-week backtest**
- ROLR and GWAV produced trades in the 2-week backtest but not here
- Need to verify env settings match exactly
- Could be a `sim_start` time difference (scanner JSON uses different start times)

### Possible V3 Approaches

1. **Remove volume minimum entirely** — let any gap-up stock through, rely on the bot's internal scoring to filter. Risk: more noise.

2. **Use "volume relative to average" instead of absolute volume** — a stock trading 10x its normal volume at 5K shares is more interesting than a stock trading 1x at 5M shares.

3. **Rank by gap% instead of volume** — gap% was the common trait of VERO, BNAI, GWAV, ROLR. They all had significant gaps.

4. **Widen to top 10-15 candidates** — more chances to catch the runner. The trade cap (5/day) still prevents overtrading.

5. **Fix the scanner** — if VERO isn't in the scanner, that's a data problem, not a ranking problem. Start here.
