## Phase B: MAX_SQ_TRADES=3 — REVERTED

### Critical Finding: VERO Has 3 SQ Trades, Not 5

The directive assumed VERO had 5 SQ trades and would still be blocked at MAX_SQ=3. **VERO actually has 3 SQ trades on IBKR tick data.** This means raising MAX_SQ from 2→3 unlocks CT on VERO — where it loses money.

### B1: Results (before revert)

| Stock | SQ-Only | SQ+CT (MAX_SQ=3) | Delta | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------------------|-------|-------|
| ROLR | +$12,601 | +$14,565 | **+$1,964** | CT added value! |
| VERO | +$562 | -$251 | **-$813** | REGRESSION — CT fired and lost |
| SHPH | +$3,115 | +$3,115 | $0 | CT didn't fire (gates rejected) |

**ROLR benefited from CT** — the post-cascade continuation at higher levels added +$1,964. But VERO was hurt — CT entries on fading pullbacks lost money.

**Decision: Reverted MAX_SQ to 2.** We can't sacrifice VERO's regression for ROLR's improvement. SHPH didn't benefit anyway (CT gates rejected all pullbacks even with 3-SQ access).

## Observations for Cowork

1. **MACD soft gate lets CT fire but picks bad entries.** ASTC's CT entries at 11:23-11:33 were during a fade. The MACD hard gate was actually protecting us from fading stocks.

2. **VERO has 3 SQ trades on IBKR data, not 5.** Previous counts may have been from Databento data. Any future MAX_SQ changes must account for this.

3. **SHPH doesn't benefit from CT regardless of settings.** Even with MAX_SQ=3 and MACD soft, CT's quality gates reject all pullbacks on SHPH. The $4→$25 move happens too fast for CT's pullback pattern to form.

4. **V2 (60-min lockout) remains the best option.** The longer wait gives stocks time to digest, and when MACD recovers naturally, CT enters at better levels. The 10-min lockout puts CT into the danger zone of active sell-offs.
