**APVO, ACCL, TWG — all collapsed:**
These stocks dipped below VWAP and NEVER came back. They spent the entire session in `severe_vwap_loss` cycles (11-38% below VWAP). The VR detector correctly stayed in BELOW_VWAP → RESET loops. These are NOT VWAP reclaim stocks — they're gap-and-fade stocks that happened to cross VWAP a few times during the initial run.

**Key insight:** The study data's "VWAP cross count" metric was misleading. A stock that crosses VWAP 9 times might be oscillating during the initial run (above VWAP), then collapsing below permanently. The VR pattern needs: run → dip → RECOVERY. Most of these stocks had run → dip → COLLAPSE.

### Root Cause Analysis

The VR detector is mechanically correct but the test stocks don't exhibit the VWAP reclaim pattern:

1. **R-cap still too tight** — GRI's $0.87 R was blocked by $0.80 cap. Need $1.00+ or percentage-based cap matching the stock's ATR.

2. **`severe_vwap_loss` reset is too aggressive** — The detector resets when price drops >5% below VWAP. On volatile small-caps, a 5-8% dip below VWAP IS the setup — that's where the reclaim opportunity begins. The reset should probably be 15-20% or disabled entirely (let `max_below_bars` handle timeouts).

3. **The test stocks were gap-and-fade, not dip-and-reclaim** — High VWAP cross count ≠ reclaim opportunity. We need stocks where:
   - Price ran above VWAP for 30+ minutes (establishing trend)
   - Dipped below VWAP for 5-15 minutes (healthy pullback)
   - RECOVERED above VWAP with renewed volume (the reclaim)
   - Continued higher after reclaim (confirming the pattern)

4. **The ideal VR stock may be rarer than expected** in micro-cap momentum. Most gap-up small-caps either: (a) run straight up without VWAP dips (squeeze/MP territory), or (b) fade below VWAP and never return. The "dip and reclaim" pattern may be more common in mid-cap stocks or later in the session (10-11 AM).

### Recommendations for Cowork

1. **Raise `severe_vwap_loss` threshold** from ~5% to 15-20%. Current threshold resets the detector on normal small-cap volatility.

2. **Raise R-cap to $1.00** or use ATR-based cap. GRI's reclaim at $0.87 was a real setup blocked by $0.07.

3. **Find stocks with ACTUAL recovery patterns** — not just high VWAP cross counts. Look for stocks where price spent 30+ minutes below VWAP then returned above with volume. This might require fetching 1m bar data and looking for the specific sequence: `above VWAP sustained → below VWAP 5-20 bars → above VWAP again with volume`.

4. **Consider that VR may not be viable for micro-cap momentum** — If the next round of testing also produces 0 trades, VR might need to be shelved in favor of refining squeeze/MP further.
