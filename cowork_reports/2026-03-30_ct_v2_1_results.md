## Analysis: Why CT Isn't Capturing Continuation

The issue isn't the lockout or cascade gate — those are working perfectly. The issue is **the quality gates are too strict for post-squeeze pullbacks on volatile small-caps:**

1. **MACD negative (hard gate):** After a squeeze, MACD frequently goes negative during the pullback. This is normal — it's the digestion phase. But CT treats it as a "dump" signal and hard-rejects. On EEIQ, this killed every potential entry.

2. **Volume too high:** Post-squeeze pullbacks on active stocks have high volume (sell-offs). The 1.5x threshold catches some but many pullbacks still fail.

3. **Below VWAP/EMA:** These are soft gates now (pause, don't reset), but if MACD rejects first, the soft gate advantage is wasted.

### Potential Fix: Relax MACD Gate

The most impactful change would be `WB_CT_REQUIRE_MACD=0` — make MACD a soft gate or disable it entirely for CT. The squeeze already confirmed the stock. A negative MACD during the pullback is expected behavior, not a disqualifier.

This is a strategy decision, not an infrastructure one. Flagging for Cowork.

## Recommendation

Two options:

**Option A: Revert to V2 settings (60-min lockout, 1-SQ gate)** — this produced +$413 on EEIQ and $0 on everything else. Simple, proven, modest upside.

**Option B: Keep V2.1 settings but relax MACD gate** — `WB_CT_REQUIRE_MACD=0`. This would let CT enter during pullbacks where MACD is negative but price is reclaiming above VWAP/EMA. Higher risk, higher potential reward.

Either way, the cascade gate at 3+ SQ trades is rock-solid and shouldn't be changed.
